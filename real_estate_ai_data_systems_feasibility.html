Ground Rules

MLS data is restricted.

IDX and MLS agreements govern how licensed data can be displayed, stored, scraped, or reused. Systems involving MLS/MIBOR need explicit permission and compliance review.

Public records are messy.

Florida public records are strong, but county-by-county formats vary. Public does not mean clean, consistent, or API-ready.

AI is not magic.

AI is excellent at language tasks, extraction, summaries, drafts, and scoring. It is not reliable for fragile real-time scraping or predicting human behavior perfectly.

01

System-by-System Analysis

1. Pre-Foreclosure Monitor

Monitor lis pendens filings, cross-reference property data, and generate prioritized distressed-owner lead lists.

BuildableMedium

Data Sources

  • County clerk lis pendens filings
  • NAL Tax Roll data
  • Estimated equity from public record history

Reality Check

This can be built, but it will not be real-time. Daily or weekly batch pulls are realistic. Court website scraping is the fragile part.

Bottlenecks

Portal redesigns, filing delays, inconsistent county systems, and rough equity estimates.

Legal / Compliance

Public court records are accessible. Outreach must be careful, especially phone outreach under TCPA rules.

Verdict

Build this. It is achievable, legally clean, and lead quality is high. Start with Okaloosa.

2. FSBO Workshop Lead System

Build a workshop funnel for FSBO sellers and potentially monitor active FSBO listings.

Partially BuildableMedium

What Works

Workshop funnels, landing pages, email follow-up, and active FSBO monitoring are realistic.

What Fails

Predicting who will become FSBO before they list is mostly fantasy. There is no reliable accessible data source for that.

Risks

Scraping Zillow or similar sites may violate terms of service. Buying FSBO leads from REDX/Vulcan7 may be cleaner.

Verdict

Build the funnel. Skip pre-list detection. Consider paid FSBO lead sources.

3. Broker Network Intelligence

Track private broker relationships, off-market whispers, and future listing opportunities.

CRM OnlyEasy

Reality Check

Private deal flow lives in relationships, not databases. AI cannot automatically detect what brokers only say privately.

Buildable Version

A relationship CRM with notes, reminders, market focus, and AI-assisted follow-up prompts.

Compliance

NAR Clear Cooperation reduced public-pocket-listing opportunities. This should stay relationship-based.

Verdict

Strip this down. Build a simple relationship CRM, not an “intelligence system.”

4. Divorce Lead System

Monitor divorce filings and identify homeowners potentially needing to sell.

Technically BuildableHigh Risk

Reality Check

Technically possible from public records, but ethically sensitive. Poor outreach can feel predatory and trigger complaints.

Better Angle

Build attorney relationships personally. Do not cold-message people referencing divorce filings.

Legal / Compliance

Public records are accessible, but outreach must avoid exploitation and reputational harm.

Verdict

Proceed carefully or skip. Better relationship-driven than automated.

5. Estate / Probate Lead System

Monitor probate filings, identify estate properties, and build executor outreach workflows.

BuildableMedium

Data Sources

  • County probate filings
  • NAL Tax Roll
  • Name matching between decedents and property owners

Reality Check

Name matching will require fuzzy logic and some manual review, but the system is realistic.

Real-World Value

High. Executors often need professional help, especially out-of-state heirs managing inherited property.

Verdict

Build this. Clean opportunity, proven niche, public data, strong seller motivation.

6. Wholesaler Network System

Aggregate wholesale opportunities, rank deals, and analyze potential ARV/spread.

Simple CRMEasy

Reality Check

The system is easy. The hard part is deal quality and accurate ARV. Without MLS comps, ARV is guesswork.

Buildable Version

Use a submission form plus Airtable/Notion and AI-assisted red flag analysis.

MLS Need

Accurate ARV generally requires MLS comparable sales data.

Verdict

Keep it simple. Relationships matter more than software here.

7. Contractor / Builder Network

Track permits and renovations that may signal future listings or active investor activity.

BuildableMedium

Data Sources

  • County or municipal building permits
  • Property ownership records
  • Builder/contractor registration data

Reality Check

Permit data is public but scattered. Many renovations mean owners are staying, not selling.

Use Case

Better as a warm prospecting list than a hot seller predictor.

Verdict

Worth building selectively. Use only where permit portals are accessible.

8. Flipper & Property Manager System

Identify portfolio owners, investor activity, and possible future inventory.

Partially BuildableMedium

Data Sources

  • NAL ownership records
  • Sunbiz entity data
  • Multiple-parcel ownership detection

Reality Check

Identifying investors is realistic. Predicting when they will sell is speculative.

Value

High for agents targeting investors. One relationship can create multiple transactions.

Verdict

Build as an extension of NAL. Great database layer; don’t oversell prediction.

9. Vacant Property Detection

Detect vacant homes and rank likely motivated sellers.

NoisyHard

Reality Check

The best vacancy signals are USPS and utility data, which are private or licensed. Public proxy signals are noisy.

Public Proxy

Absentee ownership, non-homestead, long ownership, and no recent activity can suggest vacancy — but can’t prove it.

Bottleneck

High false positives. Many absentee homes are rented, not vacant.

Verdict

Do not build separately. Add vacancy proxy signals to absentee owner scoring.

10. Absentee Owner Database

Pull county property records, detect owners living elsewhere, and rank by seller potential.

Already ProvenEasy

Status

Already built for Okaloosa. Python pipeline is reusable. Lead scoring model is defined.

Scalability

Florida’s NAL Tax Roll makes this repeatable across 67 counties with minimal adaptation.

Value

Highest of the list. Proven lead quality, low cost, scalable, and legally clean.

Verdict

This is the core asset. Scale it first.

11. AI CRM Assistant

Summarize messages, draft responses, detect neglected leads, and generate daily briefings.

BuildableMedium

Data Sources

  • Gmail or Outlook API
  • Lofty CRM or similar CRM
  • Lead activity and pipeline notes

Reality Check

AI is very good at this, but should work as a co-pilot, not autopilot.

ROI

Extremely high. Follow-up neglect loses deals. This system can recover value quickly.

Verdict

Build early. Check Lofty native AI first to avoid duplicate work.

12. Transaction Coordination AI

Read contracts, extract deadlines, track milestones, and generate closing summaries.

BuildableMedium

Reality Check

AI can reliably extract common dates and obligations, but human review is still required for unusual clauses.

Workflow

Document intake → AI extraction → calendar/task creation → deadline alerts.

Value

Very high. Missed deadlines create real liability and stress.

Verdict

Build this. Strong operational ROI and risk reduction.

13. Social Content Automation

Draft market updates, listing posts, newsletters, and neighborhood content.

BuildableEasy

Reality Check

This is the easiest AI system to build. The issue is not generation — it is making the content sound authentic.

Human Review

Do not auto-post raw AI content. Use AI drafts with human approval and personal voice.

Cost

Near zero with existing AI tools. Scheduling tools may cost $15–$25/month.

Verdict

Easiest win. Start here to show fast value.

14. Business Intelligence Dashboard

Analyze transaction history, referral trends, neighborhood performance, and production metrics.

BuildableMedium

Reality Check

Very useful when built around the agent’s own data. Competitive market benchmarking requires MLS or paid sources.

Best Starting Point

Start with personal transaction data only. A clean spreadsheet with charts may deliver 80% of the value.

Bottleneck

Historical data cleanup. Agents often have records scattered across systems.

Verdict

Worthwhile. Keep it practical and avoid dashboard overkill.

15. Predictive Seller Modeling

Predict which homeowners are statistically likely to sell soon.

Simplified OnlyHard

Reality Check

A simple scoring model is realistic. A true predictive model competitive with enterprise platforms is not realistic with public data alone.

Missing Signals

Life-stage changes, income shifts, household composition, job changes, and family events are mostly private or commercial-data-broker territory.

Better Framing

Call it “equity-rich long-term owner targeting,” not predictive modeling.

Verdict

Do not build standalone. Add variables to the absentee owner scoring model.

03

Ideas Likely to Fail in Practice

Broker Network Intelligence

The useful information lives in relationships, not structured data.

Predictive Seller Modeling

Without private life-stage data, accuracy is too low to justify complexity.

Vacant Property Detection

The best signals are private; public signals overlap with absentee-owner data.

FSBO Pre-List Detection

The data does not exist in a reliable accessible form.
